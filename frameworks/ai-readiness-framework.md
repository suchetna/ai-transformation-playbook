# AI Readiness Framework

This framework helps assess whether an organization is ready to move from AI experimentation to sustained operational use.

## 1. Strategy

Questions:
- Is there a clear reason for adopting AI?
- Are AI efforts tied to business priorities?
- Is success defined beyond vague innovation goals?

Signs of readiness:
- Clear business problems are identified
- AI initiatives support strategic priorities
- Outcomes are measurable

## 2. Leadership and ownership

Questions:
- Is there executive sponsorship?
- Is ownership clear across teams?
- Are decisions coordinated or fragmented?

Signs of readiness:
- One or more leaders are accountable
- Teams know who owns decisions
- AI work is not happening in silos

## 3. Use cases

Questions:
- Are use cases tied to real workflow problems?
- Are teams prioritizing based on value and feasibility?
- Are pilots evaluated consistently?

Signs of readiness:
- Use cases are clearly scoped
- Prioritization logic exists
- Pilots are compared using shared criteria

## 4. Workflows

Questions:
- Have workflows been mapped before AI is introduced?
- Are handoffs and review points clear?
- Is AI improving the process, not just adding another step?

Signs of readiness:
- Existing workflows are understood
- AI is integrated intentionally
- Teams know when human review is required

## 5. Governance

Questions:
- Are there clear guardrails for responsible use?
- Are data, risk, and compliance concerns addressed?
- Do teams know what should not be automated?

Signs of readiness:
- Policies exist
- High-risk use is reviewed
- Teams understand boundaries

## 6. Adoption

Questions:
- Are people trained to use AI in context?
- Are champions or internal support systems in place?
- Is adoption measured meaningfully?

Signs of readiness:
- Teams have practical guidance
- Good usage patterns are shared
- Adoption is tracked beyond tool access
