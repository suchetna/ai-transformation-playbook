# AI-Ready Operating Model Outline

This outline helps organizations define the operating model needed to move from AI experimentation to reliable, scaled use.

## 1. Strategic direction

Key questions:
- Why is the organization investing in AI?
- Which business priorities should AI support?
- What outcomes matter most over the next 12 months?

## 2. Ownership and decision-making

Key questions:
- Who owns AI strategy?
- Who approves use cases, tools, and guardrails?
- How are decisions coordinated across business, technology, and risk teams?

## 3. Use-case portfolio

Key questions:
- Which use cases are being explored, piloted, or scaled?
- How are use cases prioritized?
- What criteria determine whether a pilot moves forward?

## 4. Workflow redesign

Key questions:
- Which workflows need redesign rather than simple automation?
- Where should AI assist, and where should humans remain accountable?
- How are review points and handoffs defined?

## 5. Governance and guardrails

Key questions:
- What data, legal, compliance, and risk controls are required?
- Which uses are allowed, restricted, or prohibited?
- How are incidents or failures handled?

## 6. Enablement and adoption

Key questions:
- How are teams trained?
- Are there champions or support mechanisms?
- How are good practices captured and shared?

## 7. Measurement

Key questions:
- How is value being measured?
- What signals show real adoption?
- What indicates that AI is improving work rather than adding noise?
